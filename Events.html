Monthly Main Events

January

Hatsu-mōde

Hatsu-mōde, the first shrine visit of the new year, starts just after midnight on 1 January and continues through Shōgatsu. Meiji-jingū is the most popular spot in Tokyo; it can get very, very crowded, but that's part of the experience.

February

Plum Blossoms

Plum (ume) blossoms, which appear towards the end of the month, are the first sign that winter is ending. Popular viewing spots include Koishikawa Kōrakuen and Yushima Tenjin.

March

Tokyo Haru-sai

Month-long classical-music festival Tokyo Haru-sai is held annually over March and April at venues around Ueno-kōen. Look out for the occasional performance in unconventional spaces like the Gallery of Hōryū-ji Treasures and the National Museum of Nature & Science. Purchase tickets online

April

Nakizumo

At Sensō-ji sumo wrestlers pull faces to make babies cry, and the one who cries first, or loudest, is the winner – because in Japan it’s believed that crying babies grow big and strong. It's usually held on the last Saturday of the month.

May

Roppongi Art Night

Held in late May, this weekend-long (literally, venues stay open all night) arts event (www.roppongiartnight.com) sees large-scale installations and performances taking over the museums, galleries and streets of Roppongi. The vibe is more party than highbrow.

June

BeerFes Tokyo

Sample over 100 different craft beers from around Japan and the world – as much as you want for 3½ hours! – over the first weekend of June at Yebisu Garden Place. Tickets often sell out, get them early!

July

Sumida-gawa Fireworks

The grandest of the summer fireworks shows, held the last Saturday in July, sees an incredible 20,000 pyrotechnic wonders explode over the Sumida-gawa in Asakusa. The show starts around 7pm, but you'll have to get there way earlier to get a good spot.

August

Asakusa Samba Carnival

On the last Saturday in August, Tokyo’s Nikkei Brazilian community and local samba clubs turn Asakusa's Kaminarimon-dōri into one big party for the Asakusa Samba Carnival (www.asakusa-samba.org).

September

Moon Viewing

Full moons in September and October call for tsukimi (moon-viewing gatherings). People eat tsukimi dango – mochi, pounded rice dumplings that are round like the moon.

October

Halloween

Tokyo has gone mad for Halloween recently. Thousands of costumed merry-makers now converge on Shibuya Crossing for one big, chaotic street party over the last weekend in October. Shinjuku Ni-chōme and Roppongi are two other popular destinations, where many bars and clubs host Halloween events.

November

Kagurazaka Street Stage Ōedo Tour

Traditional music and storytelling in the streets of atmospheric Kagurazaka (http://kaguramachi.jp) and its shrine, Akagi-jinja; there's even a cameo appearance by the neighbourhood's geisha.

December

Winter Illuminations

Tokyo loves its winter illuminations, and commercial districts like Ginza outdo themselves with extravagant displays throughout December. Keiyaki-zaka at Roppongi Hills is particularly magical.
